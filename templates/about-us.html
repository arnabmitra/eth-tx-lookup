Why Gamma Exposure Matters

Gamma exposure represents the sensitivity of market makers' delta hedging to price changes. When dealers have large gamma positions, they're forced to buy or sell the underlying asset as prices move, creating self-reinforcing price action.

Understanding GEX levels helps traders:

  • Identify key support and resistance levels where dealer hedging activity concentrates
  • Anticipate periods of low vs. high volatility based on market maker positioning
  • Recognize "pinning" behavior where stocks gravitate toward high GEX strikes at expiration
  • Understand when markets are likely to trend vs. chop in a range
  • Make more informed decisions about option strategies and directional trades
